Background



For measuring glucose level the most popular method is invasive glucometers.As the name suggests they are invasive, which means painful and inconvenient for the person because it needs a blood sample therefore penetrating the skin is necessary. They use strips that are expensive and need to be replaced each time of use which can be unaffordable.Another issue is, using the same needles for several penetrations has a high possibility of entering infections into your blood.

Our aim in this project is to eliminate these drawbacks and implement a convenient system for frequent glucose monitoring.





What do you think the world's fastest-growing chronic disease is?It's Diabetes.According to the International Diabetes Federation 1 in 11 adults suffer from diabetes. That is 425 Mil of World's population.Severe diabetes can cause serious complications like stroke, amputation, kidney failure and heart attack. And also every 6 seconds dies from Diabetes. Therefore, it should be monitored by measuring blood glucose levels regularly and keeping them under control.

Our Solution



According to the recent research conducted on this subject, three methods have been found to measure glucose non invasively and the method that we are focusing is called the Near Infrared based method. The concept is that when a near infrared light ray is sent through the finger tip, it will get absorbed and scattered inside the finger and the output light ray will have a loss of intensity. It is observed that when there is low glucose concentration, low scattering and hence attenuation of the ray is high causing intensity to increase while for a less glucose concentration the opposite happens. Based on this and many more theories such as the Beer Lambart law, scientists have come to a final conclusion that there is a correlation between the glucose concentration and the intensity of the output light. Based on this, we can build a regression formula and use it to calculate glucose concentration at any output intensity
